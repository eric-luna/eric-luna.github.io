Hayao Miyazaki

Film Director, Producer, Screenwriter, Animator

Hayao Miyazaki is one of Japan's greatest animation directors. The entertaining plots, compelling characters, and breathtaking animation
in his films have earned him international renown from critics as well as public recognition within Japan. The Walt Disney Company's commitment
to introduce the films to the rest of the world will let more people appreciate the high-quality works he has given the movie-going public.

Miyazaki's Early Animated Works

  • 1968 – Hols: Prince of the Sun – Key animation, storyboards, scene design
  • 1969 – The Wonderful World of Puss 'n Boots - Key animation, storyboards, design
  • 1969 – Flying Phantom Ship – Key animation, storyboards, design
  • 1970 – Moomin – Key animation on the Mushi Production version
  • 1971 – Animal Treasure Island – Story consultant, key animation, storyboards, scene design
  • 1971 – Ali Baba and the Forty Thieves – Organizer, key animation, storyboards
  • 1971 – Lupin III Part I (TV series) – Direction, along with Isao Takahata
  • 1972 – Yuki's Sun (Pilot for a never-realized TV series) – Direction
  • 1972 – Panda! Go, Panda! – Concept, screenplay, storyboards, scene design, key animation; direction by Isao Takahata
  • 1973 – Panda! Go, Panda! The Rainy-Day Circus - Screenplay, storyboards, scene design, art design, key animation; direction by Isao Takahata
  • 1974 – Heidi, Girl of the Alps (TV series) – Scene design, layout; direction by Isao Takahata
  • 1978 – 3000 Leagues in Search of Mother (TV series) – Scene design, layout
  • 1978 – Future Boy Conan (TV series) – Direction
  • 1979 – Anne of Green Gables (TV series) – Scene design and layout for episodes 1–15; direction by Isao Takahata
  • 1980 – Lupin III Part II (TV series) – Direction of 2 episodes under the pseudonym "Tsutomu Teruki"
  • 1980 – The New Adventures of Gigantor (TV series) - Key animation (episode 8)
  • 1984 – Sherlock Hound (TV series) – Direction
