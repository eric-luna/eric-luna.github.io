Nature & Wildlife

(Beautiful view from the Japanese Tea Garden)

Though, San Antonio is one of the largest metropolitan areas in America, it still has plenty of destinations for the nature and wildlife lover.

San Antonio Zoo

The San Antonio Zoo is a non-profit organization and is home to a wide variety of animals representing several hundred species from aroundthe world

Japanese Tea Garden

The Japanese Tea Garden has a glorious history of over 90 years, from the time it was an operating rock quarry to today, as one of the most loved, educational and cultural resources in San Antonio.

Natural Bridge Caverns

One of the top attractions in Texas, Natural Bridge Caverns features underground tours and other adventures. The Natural Bridge Caverns are the largest known commercial caverns in the U.S. state of Texas.

Wildlife Ranch

Natural Bridge Wildlife Ranch has taken to heart the care of animals from all over the world for over 30 years. With over 450-acres of pristine, natural, protected, environment, animal populations continue to thrive. Showcasing and sharing amazing creatures from all over the world. One part education, one part preservation; mixing it up for a day full of fun and adventure.

Go Tubing

San Antonio has many nearby rivers in the surrounding areas. Grad some friends and cooler and head out to one them to go for a tubing adventure.
