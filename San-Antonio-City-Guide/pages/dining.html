Dining & Drinks

(Diners enjoying a nice meal at the famous Mi Tierra)

The city's dining experience is very diverse and very cultured. There are a number of high quality restaurants throughout the city. Ranging from fancy five stars to equally delicious food trucks.

Mi Tierra

A San Antonio landmark. Mi Tierra Cafe Y Panaderia is open 24 hours, serving locals and tourists delicious mexican food.

Chris Madrid's

Chris Madrid's is a local, family-owned burger joint serving classic burgers and nachos since 1977. Lunch and dinner served six days a week.

Big Lou's Pizza

Big Lou's award-winning pizza has been a San Antonio favorite for over 10 years, but Big Lou's does more than just pizza. Make sure to try out their wraps, pasta dinners, sandwiches, and salads.

The Big Bib

In true Texas-style, The Big Bib's barbecue is slow-cooked for up to 14 hours every day with aged oak and mesquite woods. This time-consuming process gives the meat a pink tinge called a "smoke ring" and is a prized attribute in barbecue meats. In beef the ring is a reddish-pink and in pork, chicken and turkey it's bright pink.

Mezcaleria Mixtli

The Mezcaleria, located at the Yard shopping center in the Olmos Park neighborhood in central San Antonio, focuses on Mexican-centric flavors and features Mexican spirits such as tequila and the largest variety of mezcals you'll find in the city. If you're wondering what a mezcal is then you've been missing out!
